Patterns in the Night

How prostitution in San Francisco has shifted across streets, hours, and decades

Assignment 2 for 02806 Social Data Analysis and Visualization, Spring 2026 by Emilie Vad Johannsen, Emil Bagge and Emma Astely

In San Fransico, USA, prostitution is illegal. But did you know, that before 2023 the police in San Fransisco (SF) could also arrest people in the street for the act of loitering? (Local News Matters, 2023, para. 2). This law intended to get sex workers off the street since “loitering” in a public space with the “intent to commit prostitution" was criminalized (Local News Matters, 2023, para. 2). In January 2023 the loitering was decriminalized, backed up by sex workers and other minority groups, arguing that the police was enforcing discriminatory behavior (Local News Matters, 2023, para. 2). Around the same time loitering was decriminalized, San Fransico Police Department (SFPD) installed barricades on the Capp Street corridor (a residential area in SF, experiencing a high number of sex workers), intended to nagivate sex workers and clients away from the area. A question then arises - has the installation of barricades removed the sex workers from the streets or have they gone to other neighborhoods to earn their money?

In order to answer the question raised above we have investigated data from SFPD from 2003 - 2025. The data covers police reports of different incident categories. Two datasets have been merged (data from 2003-2018 and 2018-2025), cleaned up and eight focus incident categories have been chosen. The chosen focus categories are assault, burglary, drug/alcohol offense, larceny theft, prostitution, robbery, vehicle theft and warrant. The two datasets, that has been merged into one, can be found here: (dataset_2003_to_2018). and (dataset_2018_to_2025).
