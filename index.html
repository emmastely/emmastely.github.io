Patterns in the Night

How prostitution in San Francisco has shifted across streets, hours, and decades

Article by Emilie, Emil, and Emma

By exploring data from the San Fransisco Police Department and comparing data of arrests across the last 20 years we see how patterns across neigborhoods and hours cange revealing how the landscape of sexwork has changed. It reveals how the physical arena changes but how the work persists.

This story traces those movements, using police records from 2005 to 2025 to understand when and where activity becomes visible — and when it fades.

In the early 2000s, activity clustered in a small number of districts, forming consistent geographic patterns.
Over time, those patterns began to shift, spreading across new areas and becoming less predictable.
By the 2020s, the geography changes again — shaped by policy, technology, and enforcement strategies.

The story of prostitution in San Francisco is not one of disappearance, but of adaptation. Patterns emerge, dissolve, and reappear — shaped by forces both visible and hidden.
